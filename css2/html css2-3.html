Briards

A HEART WRAPPED IN FUR

The briard, or berger de brie, is a large breed of dif traditionally used as a herder and guardian of sheep.

Breed History

The briard, which is beautiful is beleived to have originated in France, has been bred for centuris to hers and to protect sheep. The breed was used by the Frech Army as sentries, messengers and to search for wounded soldiers because of its fine sense of hearing. Briards were used in the First World War almost to the point of extinction. Currenty the population of briards is slowly recovering. Charlemagne, Napoleon, Thomas Jefferson and Lafayette all owned briards.
